Unit 1, Outcome 3 (Review)
Develop and test solutions to improve ongoing use of IT tools and systems



3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency – could you have been more efficient when making your website? How? Could you have done it in teams?

Where the methods such as Kanban, hence the use of Trello, stand ups etc is found to be ineffective, it is usually due to a lack of knowledge of the fundamental lean concepts and hence the purpose. This tends to result in a lack of engagement and hence benefit.
The application of the ‘Lean Agile’ principles as defined in methodologies such as SAFe [11] significantly enhance collaboration and effectiveness and are supported by a range of proprietary IT environments.
Concepts such as continuous integration are required for complex environments, where multiple developers are iterating the software concurrently. This is the practice of merging the working versions of individual developer’s code to a shared mainline at frequent intervals i.e. several times a day. Tools such as Bamboo support this and integrate with workflow environments such as Jira to ensure effective collaboration.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency There is a paradox between productivity, i.e. effectiveness and the efficiency paradigm. Traditional management targets high utilisation as a measure of efficiency, but this frequently results in a lot of ‘work in progress’ and long queues.
The art of productivity relies on effective prioritisation and innovation. The emphasis needs to be changed to achieving high business impact by producing valuable outcomes with the minimum amount of activity.
The visibility and limitation of work in progress afforded by the Kanban boards also plays a significant role in this respect.

3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects – what would you do differently next time? Team work? Use of Slack?

Whilst this was an individual project, both the productivity and quality of the resultant website would have been enhanced by increased collaboration in the form of more regular demonstration and feedback.
Slack provides a valuable platform for collaboration, but as suggested in the agile manifesto[16], there is no substitute for face to face contact.
In this environment, the peer review would need to be based on mutual benefit and respect given the constrained time frame.

3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions

There are various strategies for testing software. These can be categorised as ‘functional’, where the software is tested against the requirements to ensure it provides the desired outcomes and ‘non functional’ tests which focus on the operational aspects[22].
Functional tests include:
  • Unit testing
  • Integration testing
  • System testing
  • Acceptance testing
Examples of non functional tests include:
  • Performance testing
  • Security testing
  • Usability testing
  • Compatibility testing

The testing of the website was focused on the functional tests, in particular a simplified form of unit tests was performed to ensure the individual components the software code performed as intended. This included checking the operation of the links, presentation of images and compatibility with alternative browsers. A limited amount of user testing was conducted by demonstrating the solution to other students and responding to feedback.
Where changes were made, regression tests were performed to ensure the existing code wasn’t impaired.
In more complex environments, this type of testing is automated to accelerate the tests and reduce errors.
